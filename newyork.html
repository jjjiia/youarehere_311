This map visualizes 168,491 noise complaints recorded by NYC's 311 service between 2010 - 2013.

New York City is full of noise, and we have learned to live with most of it. This map shows the noise that we consider to be out of the ordinary or unpleasant enough to try to stop. The noise complaints are categorized into human and machine noises. In our categorization, human noise is made up of Loud Music, Parties, Loud Talking, and Car or Truck Music. Machine noise is made up of Construction Equipment, Jack Hammering, and Construction Before or After Hours. For New York City as a whole, the noise that generates complaints are 72% Human and 29% Machine.

Machine noise indicates construction, and these occur at a high concentration in downtown Brooklyn and all over Manhattan, especially in Midtown. Queens is the borough that has the least amount of machine noise complaints, roughly 10 percent. For any specific location, human noises tend to be more frequent, as loud people are likely to be loud over a long period of time.

Click on the choices under "Filter By" to filter by human or machine noise. To see aggregated borough data, rollover borough. To see detailed location data such as address and description of the noise and the type of location, rollover bar graph.

Noise complaints are recorded by the 311 service in New York City. These are made public through the gov.data initiative and can be found at Socrata NYC Open Data . The dataset in its entirety contains all 311 service requests from the infrastructural (road repair, traffic lights, electrical outages) to isolated incidents (heating, lead testing, graffiti removal and noise complaints), as long as they directly effect the everyday lives of citizens. We isolated the noise complaint requests from this larger dataset.
